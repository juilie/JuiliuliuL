I don't really want to write today
But I am anyway to keep a habit
It's probably the family trauma
That never stops changing and adapting

I could easily spend this morning wondering
Why don't I want to write? Is it boring now?
And I'd be denying myself the space to acknowledge
The supremely obvious explanation

My mind wandered this morning
If I should tell my dad that we know everything he did
And that I still want to forgive him
And I let my brain simulate what would happen
And he beat me to death

So when I wonder why I don't want to write
I should remember there's no need to wonder
I'm doing a disservice to myself
To think it's anything but obvious
